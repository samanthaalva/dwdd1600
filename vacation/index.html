Escalante: Your Dream Vacation

Escalante has a long and rich history, making it an ideal destination for campers and tourists alike. From twisting canyons, to luxury hotels, to ideal primitive camping spots, this location offers something for everyone. At the Escalante Travel Agency, we aim to streamline the process of booking and traveling to this natural wonder. Everyone deserves to experience the gifts of Mother Nature without stress: why let it damper your camping experience?

We hope to assist campers and tourists alike in finding hotels, navigating camping areas, and locating the most enjoyable locations to enrich experiences and build everlasting memories. Popular locations include the Devil’s Garden and Metate Arch, the Hole-in-the-Rock Road, and a multitude of beautiful Slot Canyons to explore. This is all accessible from Escalante City, a small town located along Utah’s Byway 12. They hold roughly 850 residents and hope bring a “unique charm” to their corner of Southern Utah.

Escalante was named after Silvestre Velez de Escalante, the first European explorer in this region and a Fransiscan missionary. His journey is usually called “the Dominguez-Escalante Expedition”. It’s important to note that him and his companions were some of the first white men to traverse Utah’s beautiful landscapes.

During the Black Hawk Indian War of the 1860’s, individuals from the Southern Utah Militia passed through the Escalante area. They located wild potatoes in the area and named a valley, east of the Escalante Mountains, Potato Valley. After investigating Escalante in 1872, a group of settlers from Panquitch met with members from the John Westly Powell expedition. Powell’s group suggested honoring Silvestre Velez de Escalante by founding Escalante in his name. Escalante was finally settled in 1875.
